PATIENT MEDICAL RECORD
====================

PATIENT VISIT NOTES - Dr. Sarah Johnson
Date: 2023-11-15

Patient presented with complaints of persistent cough for 2 weeks, fever, and fatigue. Initial examination showed elevated temperature of 100.4°F, blood pressure 130/85, and respiratory rate of 22 breaths per minute.

DIAGNOSTIC TESTS:
- Chest X-ray: Showed infiltrates in the right lower lobe consistent with pneumonia
- Complete Blood Count: WBC 12,500/μL (elevated), indicating infection
- Blood Cultures: Negative for bacterial growth
- COVID-19 PCR Test: Negative
- Sputum Culture: Positive for Streptococcus pneumoniae, sensitive to amoxicillin

DIAGNOSIS:
Community-acquired pneumonia (right lower lobe), bacterial etiology (Streptococcus pneumoniae)

TREATMENT:
- Admitted for IV antibiotics and supportive care
- Started on IV Ceftriaxone 1g every 24 hours
- Supplemental oxygen via nasal cannula at 2L/min to maintain O2 saturation >95%
- Acetaminophen 650mg every 6 hours as needed for fever and pain
- IV fluids for hydration

HOSPITAL COURSE:
Day 1-2: Patient remained febrile with persistent cough. Oxygen requirements stable.
Day 3: Fever resolved, improved appetite, decreased oxygen requirements.
Day 4-5: Significant clinical improvement. Transitioned from IV to oral antibiotics (Amoxicillin-Clavulanate 875mg/125mg twice daily).
Day 6: Patient ambulating independently, vital signs stable, no oxygen requirement.

MEDICATIONS AT DISCHARGE:
1. Amoxicillin-Clavulanate 875mg/125mg, 1 tablet twice daily for 7 days
2. Acetaminophen 650mg every 6 hours as needed for pain
3. Dextromethorphan-Guaifenesin syrup 10mL every 4 hours as needed for cough

FOLLOW-UP RECOMMENDATIONS:
- Follow-up appointment with primary care physician in 1 week
- Repeat chest X-ray in 4-6 weeks to ensure resolution
- Rest and gradually increase activity as tolerated
- Maintain good hydration
- Return to work/school after completing 7 days of antibiotics if symptoms continue to improve

PREVENTIVE MEASURES:
- Recommended pneumococcal vaccination at follow-up visit
- Annual influenza vaccination
- Smoking cessation counseling provided (patient is a current smoker, 1/2 pack per day for 10 years)
